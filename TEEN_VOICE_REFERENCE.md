## Slang Dictionary Usage

### High Frequency (30%+ usage)
- **literally** - Intensifier, used by dramatic/outgoing personalities
  - "literally the worst day ever"
  - "literally so tired rn"

- **rn** (right now) - Urgency marker
  - "so tired rn"
  - "can't deal with this rn"

- **ngl** (not gonna lie) - Honesty signal
  - "ngl this is kinda fun"
  - "that test was hard ngl"

- **bro** - Casual address, athletic/chill personalities
  - "bro just teach"
  - "bro what even is this"

### Medium Frequency (20-25% usage)
- **fr fr** (for real for real) - Affirmation
  - "lunch table has main character energy fr fr"

- **no cap** - Truth assertion
  - "best friend ever no cap"

- **the way** - Story starter, dramatic personalities
  - "the way you were being so rude"
  - "the way i forgot my homework"

- **tbh** (to be honest) - Sincere statement
  - "tbh i didn't study"

### Lower Frequency (15-20% usage)
- **lowkey** - Downplayer
  - "lowkey kinda nervous"
  - "lowkey that test was easy"

- **highkey** - Bold statement
  - "highkey the best day"

- **slay** - Celebration/empowerment
  - "95% on the test!! SLAY"

- **periodt** - Final statement, sassy personalities
  - "fake friends are worse than no friends periodt"

- **iykyk** (if you know you know) - Insider reference
  - "Jordan when i said the thing ðŸ’€ðŸ’€ iykyk"

### Specialized (Personality-specific)
- **its giving** - Observational, trendy personalities
  - "its giving main character energy"

- **nvm** (never mind) - Distracted personalities
  - "wait nvm i found it"

- **imo** (in my opinion) - Thoughtful personalities
  - "imo homework is too much"

## Capitalization Rules

### ALL CAPS
**When to use:**
- Mood: excited or angry
- Energy: >80
- Personality: dramatic, outgoing (when excited)

**Examples:**
```
"FIELD TRIP TOMORROW IM HYPED"
"WHY SO MUCH HOMEWORK"
"SURPRISE QUIZ?? YOU CANNOT BE SERIOUS RN"

### all lowercase
**When to use:**

- Personality: shy, introverted

```

"just want a quiet day today..."

## Authenticity Tips

### What Makes It Real

1. **Inconsistency** - Real kids don't have perfect grammar
2. **Emotion** - Posts reflect genuine feelings
3. **Context** - Posts respond to actual events
4. **Personality** - Voice matches character consistently
5. **Trends** - Uses current (2024-2025) slang appropriately

### What to Avoid

1. âŒ Perfect grammar and spelling
2. âŒ Adult vocabulary ("indeed", "furthermore", "consequently")
3. âŒ Overly thoughtful/deep posts (unless studious personality)
4. âŒ No emoji (except shy students)
5. âŒ Outdated slang ("yolo", "swag", "bae")

### Age-Appropriate Considerations

**5th graders (10-11 years old) are:**
- Still learning internet culture
- Less edgy than middle schoolers
- More earnest, less ironic
- Still excited about "basic" things
- Not fully fluent in all current slang

**Keep posts:**
- School-appropriate
- Innocent in nature
- Enthusiastic about small things
- Socially focused (friends, events, feelings)
- Less cynical than older teens
